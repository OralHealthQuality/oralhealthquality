The **Texas Center for Oral Healthcare Quality and Safety** at UTHealth Houston is a research center dedicated to improving the quality and safety of dental care through informatics, measurement, and evidence-based tools. The Center is directed by **Dr. Muhammad F. Walji**, with faculty appointments at the McWilliams School of Biomedical Informatics and the School of Dentistry.

## Mission

We believe oral health care should be as safe, measurable, and evidence-based as the rest of health care. We develop the data resources, quality measures, diagnostic standards, and clinical tools that make that possible — and we share them openly so others can use and build on them.

## What we do

Our research program spans the full arc of oral health quality and safety:

- **Diagnostic safety** — standardized dental diagnostic terminology and the study of diagnostic failures.
- **Patient safety** — defining, detecting, and reducing adverse events in dental care.
- **Quality measurement** — electronic clinical quality measures (dental eMeasures) and their implementation.
- **Dental data** — the BigMouth multi-institution dental data repository.
- **Patient-reported outcomes** — understanding pain and experience after dental procedures.
- **Stewardship** — reducing inappropriate antibiotic and opioid prescribing in dentistry.
- **Artificial intelligence** — AI-driven diagnostics and decision support for periodontitis.

## A distributed, multi-institution effort

Our projects are conducted with collaborators across many institutions, including UTHealth Houston, and partners at universities, health systems, and practice-based research networks nationally. This breadth lets our findings and tools reflect real-world dental care across diverse settings.

## Funding

This work is supported by the National Institutes of Health (NIDCR, NLM), the Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality (AHRQ), and other sponsors. Specific awards are listed on each [project page](/projects/).
