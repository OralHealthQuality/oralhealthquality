## Goal

Dental caries remains the most common chronic disease worldwide, yet a large gap persists between what the evidence recommends for caries prevention and management and what happens in everyday practice. This project closes that gap by packaging caries risk assessment and disease-management guidance into usable tools and studying how they change care.

## Approach

The team examines how caries risk assessment is documented and used, identifies gaps in caries prevention and management across institutions, and develops toolkit resources — risk-assessment workflows, decision support, and patient materials — that fit into routine clinical practice.

## Impact

By making evidence-based caries management practical, this work aims to shift dentistry from a drill-and-fill model toward true disease prevention and management.
