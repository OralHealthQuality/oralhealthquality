## Goal

Much of what matters most to patients — pain, function, and the lived experience of dental care — is invisible in the clinical record. This project develops dental patient-reported outcomes (dPROs) and uses them to understand outcomes that only patients can report.

## Approach

*Understanding Pain after Dental Procedures* (UH3 DE029158), conducted within the National Dental Practice-Based Research Network, prospectively measures postoperative pain across common dental procedures and the factors that drive it, including the role of opioid and non-opioid management. The work also pilots mobile health tools for capturing acute postoperative pain in real time.

## Impact

Better measurement of pain and patient experience informs safer prescribing, shared decision-making, and a more patient-centered standard of dental care.
