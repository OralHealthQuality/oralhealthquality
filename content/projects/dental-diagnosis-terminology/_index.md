## Goal

Dentistry has historically recorded treatments far more consistently than diagnoses. Without structured, standardized diagnostic information in the dental record, it is difficult to know whether care is appropriate, to study outcomes, or to detect when a diagnosis was missed or wrong. This project develops and validates standardized dental diagnostic terminology and the electronic health record tools that make it usable at the point of care.

## Approach

Building on the Dental Diagnostic System (DDS) and its integration into academic and private practice EHRs, the team studies how clinicians actually use diagnostic terms, validates the completeness and accuracy of recorded diagnoses, and characterizes the nature and burden of diagnostic failures in dentistry. The AHRQ-funded *Diagnostic Failures in Dentistry* study (R01 HS027938) examines where and why diagnostic errors occur and how they affect patients.

## Impact

Standardized diagnostic data underpins quality measurement, patient safety surveillance, and learning health systems across the rest of our portfolio. The terminology and findings inform EHR vendors, dental schools, and quality organizations working to make diagnosis-driven dentistry the norm.
