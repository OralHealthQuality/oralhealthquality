## Goal

Patient safety is far less studied in dentistry than in medicine, yet dental care carries real risks of harm. This project builds the methods, systems, and evidence base needed to make dental care measurably safer — defining what counts as a dental adverse event, quantifying how often harm occurs, and identifying who is most affected.

## Approach

The work spans three complementary efforts: developing a dental patient safety reporting and learning system (R01 DE022628), measuring the occurrence of and disparities in dental clinic adverse events (R01 HS024406), and the Open Wide Learning Lab (OWLL), which improves patient safety in dentistry through a collaborative learning network (R18 HS027268). Together these established shared definitions of adverse events, trigger-tool methods to find them in records, and studies of safety culture and clinician burnout.

## Impact

Findings have produced consensus definitions of dental adverse events, practical tools for safety surveillance, and a growing community of practice — giving dental organizations the means to detect harm and act on it.
