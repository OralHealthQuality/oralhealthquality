## Goal

Dentists are among the top prescribers of antibiotics, and a substantial share of dental antibiotic prescriptions are unnecessary — contributing to antimicrobial resistance and avoidable harm. This project applies informatics to understand why inappropriate prescribing happens and to test interventions that reduce it.

## Approach

*Informatics Approaches to Understand and Reduce Inappropriate Antibiotic Prescribing by Dentists* (R01 DE032368, multiple-PI with Drs. D. Brad Rindal and Katie Suda) characterizes prescribing patterns in large dental datasets, identifies the clinical situations driving unnecessary prescriptions, and designs audit-and-feedback dashboards that help dentists see and improve their own prescribing. Related work extends these methods to opioid prescribing.

## Impact

The project advances antimicrobial and opioid stewardship in dentistry, giving practices and policymakers evidence-based tools to curb inappropriate prescribing.
